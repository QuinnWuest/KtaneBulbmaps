There are two 6×6 grids of bulbs on the module. Use the switch at the right to toggle between the two grids.

  • Each bulb can be either lit or unlit.
  • Each bulb can either be transparent or opaque.
  • Each bulb can be colored red, green, blue, magenta, yellow, or white.

All eight quadrants across the two bulbmaps will be converted into a binary string with a length of eight.

In the order of red bulbs, green bulbs, blue bulbs, magenta bulbs, yellow bulbs, white bulbs, lit bulbs, and transparent bulbs, set each bit in the binary string to a one if there is an odd number of said properties within the given quadrant, or a zero otherwise.

Place the binary strings into the rows of the 8×8 grid. The order of the binary strings are derived from:

  • The top-left quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to O.
  • The top-right quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to O.
  • The bottom-left quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to O.
  • The bottom-right quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to O.
  • The bottom-right quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to I.
  • The bottom-left quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to I.
  • The top-right quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to I.
  • The top-left quadrant of the bulbmap when the switch is flipped to I.

In the table on the next page, start at the row equal to the first letter in the serial number, and analyze the condition.

If it is true, find the calculated number for that condition, keeping all answers between 1 and 6 by repeatedly adding or subtracting 6 until it falls within that range. Press the button equal to that calculation.

Continue through the remaining conditions, going down the table. (or up if there is an odd number of batteries)

Pressing the button whose label is equal to the first instance of a repetition of a calculated number will disarm the module.
